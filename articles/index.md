# Articles

### Tutorial

- [1. The board and the five
  axes](https://r-heller.github.io/multixoR/articles/tutorial-1-the-board.md):

  Where multixoR is played: the 4x4x4 spatial cube, the cell index, and
  the two extra axes - time and timeline - that make the game
  five-dimensional.

- [2. Playing your first
  game](https://r-heller.github.io/multixoR/articles/tutorial-2-first-game.md):

  Turn order, present moves, the cell index in action, reading the board
  after every ply, and the canonical move notation.

- [3. Branching into the
  past](https://r-heller.github.io/multixoR/articles/tutorial-3-branching.md):

  The branch move: placing into a past board to spawn a new parallel
  timeline, and visualising the resulting multiverse with the overview
  and tree plots.

- [4. Winning across space, time and
  timelines](https://r-heller.github.io/multixoR/articles/tutorial-4-winning.md):

  The win condition, the five classes of winning line, a worked spatial
  win and a worked timeline win, the placement-anchored rule, and
  reading threats.

- [5. Strategy, evaluation and
  AI](https://r-heller.github.io/multixoR/articles/tutorial-5-strategy-ai.md):

  Reading positions with the heuristic evaluator and win probability,
  rating every legal move, and the search and MCTS engines behind the
  built-in AI.

- [6. Playing in the Shiny
  app](https://r-heller.github.io/multixoR/articles/tutorial-6-the-app.md):

  A guided, screenshot-by-screenshot tour of the bundled Shiny app:
  launching it, the control sidebar, and the five analysis tabs.
